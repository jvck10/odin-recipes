Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna? Possibly one of the most loved foods in the whole world and understandably so! The sight of lasagna evokes such a sentimental and comforting feeling, warm savoury deliciousness! This recipe is simple and easy, delicious and filling!

Ingredients

Lasagna is made up of 3 main components! Those are:

  1. Meat Sauce
  2. The White Sauce
  3. Pasta & Cheese

    For the Meat Sauce you'll need:

  • 1tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 1 rib/stick of celery
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1kg/2lb beef mince
  • 800g/28oz crushed tomato
  • 1/4C tomato paste
  • 3 beef bouillon cubes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2t each dried thyme & oregano
  • 2T Wrocestershire sauce
  • 1-2t sugar
  • 1/2t salt & black pepper

    • For the White Sauce:

  • 4T butter
  • 1/2C flour
  • 4C milk
  • 2C shredded cheese
  • pinch freshly ground nutmeg
  • salt and pepper

    • Lastly you'll need:

  • 350g Lasagna sheets
  • 1 1/2C shredded mozzarella cheese
  • finely chopped basil

These are the Steps to making your masterpiece of a lasagna!

    Meat Sauce:

  1. Heat oil in a large heavy based pot over medium heat. Add garlic, onion, celery and carrots. Cook for 10 minutes until softened and sweet - they should not brown (if they do, turn heat down).
  2. Add beef, turn heat up and cook the beef, breaking it up as you go.
  3. Once the beef has all turned brown, add the remaining Ragu ingredients EXCEPT the sugar.
  4. Stir then adjust the heat so it is bubbling very gently. Place the lid on and cook for 1.5 - 2 hours, stirring every now and then, then remove the lid and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. The ragu is ready when the meat is really tender and the sauce has thickened and is rich - see video for consistency (Note 6). Adjust salt and pepper to taste, and add sugar if required (Note 3)

    6. White Sauce

  6. Warm milk up in a saucepan (optional - just makes sauce thicken faster).
  7. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium low heat. Add flour and mix constantly for 1 minute.
  8. Pour about 1 cup of the milk in, mixing as you go to incorporate into the flour mixture. Once mostly lump free, add remaining milk. Use a whisk if needed to make it lump free.
  9. Turn heat up to medium high. Stir occasionally at first then regularly after a few minutes until sauce thickens - about 5 - 8 minutes. It should coat the back of the wooden spoon.
  10. Remove from heat, add cheese, nutmeg, salt and pepper. Mix until the cheese is melted. The Sauce should be thick but still easily pourable - the consistency of heavy cream (you need to be able to drizzle it over the Ragu when layering - see video). If it's too thick, add a splash of water or milk.

    11. Put it together!

  11. Preheat oven to 180°C/350°F.
  12. Use a 33 x 22 x 7 cm / 13 x 9 x 2.5" baking dish.
  13. Smear a bit of Ragu on the base, then cover with lasagna sheets. Tear sheets to fit.
  14. Spread over 2 1/2 cups of Ragu (enough to cover sheets), then drizzle over 1 cup of Cheese Sauce.
  15. Top with lasagna sheets (Note 7). Spread with another 2 1/2 cups of Ragu, then 1 cup of Cheese Sauce. Top with lasagna sheets then repeat 1 more time.
  16. Top with a 4th layer of lasagna sheets, then pour over the remaining Cheese Sauce.
  17. Sprinkle with Mozzarella, then bake for 25 minutes or until golden and bubbling.
  18. Stand for 5 to 10 minutes before cutting and serving, garnished with basil or parsley if desired.
